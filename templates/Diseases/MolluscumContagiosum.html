Molluscum Contagiosum

Overview

Molluscum contagiosum is a common skin condition caused by a virus that results in small, raised, pearl-like bumps on the skin. The virus is contagious and can be spread through skin-to-skin contact or by sharing towels or other personal items. The bumps usually disappear on their own within 6-12 months, but treatment may be recommended to prevent the spread of the virus.

Symptoms

  • Small, raised, pearl-like bumps on the skin
  • Bumps have a central indentation or "core"
  • Bumps can occur anywhere on the body, except for the palms of the hands and the soles of the feet
  • Bumps may be itchy, sore, or red

Treatment

Treatment options for molluscum contagiosum include:

  • Physical removal of the bumps through scraping, freezing, or laser therapy
  • Application of topical creams or ointments
  • Prescription medications, such as imiquimod or cidofovir
