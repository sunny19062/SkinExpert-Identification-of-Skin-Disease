Drug Eruptions

Overview

Drug eruptions are a skin reaction that occurs after exposure to medication. Symptoms range from mild rash to severe blistering or even life-threatening anaphylaxis. Medications that most commonly cause drug eruptions include antibiotics, anti-epileptics, and nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs). Treatment may involve stopping the offending medication, applying topical creams or ointments, and taking oral corticosteroids.

Symptoms

  • rash
  • hives
  • blistering
  • itching
  • redness

Treatment

Treatment may involve stopping the offending medication, applying topical creams or ointments, and taking oral corticosteroids.
