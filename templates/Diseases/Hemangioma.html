Hemangioma

Overview

Hemangioma is a non-cancerous growth of blood vessels that can occur anywhere in the body, but is most common on the skin or liver. In most cases, hemangiomas are not harmful and do not require treatment. However, if a hemangioma is causing symptoms or is in a location that could affect an organ's function, treatment may be necessary.

Symptoms

  • red or purple skin lesion
  • raised or flat appearance
  • rapid growth during the first 6 months
  • gradual fading over the next several years

Treatment

Treatment may not be necessary if the hemangioma is not causing symptoms and is not in a location that could affect an organ's function. However, treatment options include medication, laser therapy, and surgery.
