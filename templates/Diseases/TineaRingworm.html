Tinea (Ringworm)

Overview

Tinea, commonly known as ringworm, is a fungal infection that affects the skin and nails. It is characterized by a red, itchy rash in a circular or ring-like shape. The infection is most commonly found on the scalp, feet, groin, and nails. Treatment typically involves antifungal creams, powders, or oral medication.

Symptoms

  • Red, itchy rash in a circular or ring-like shape
  • Dry, scaly, or cracked skin
  • Blisters or pus-filled bumps
  • Thickened, discolored, or brittle nails (in case of nail infection)

Treatment

Treatment typically involves antifungal creams, powders, or oral medication.
