Exanthem

Overview

An exanthem is a widespread rash that is usually accompanied by systemic symptoms such as fever and malaise. Exanthems can be caused by a variety of infectious and non-infectious agents, including viruses, bacteria, drugs, and toxins. The appearance of the rash can vary depending on the cause and may be maculopapular, vesicular, or petechial. Treatment depends on the underlying cause of the exanthem.

Symptoms

  • widespread rash
  • fever
  • malaise
  • maculopapular rash
  • vesicular rash
  • petechial rash

Treatment

Treatment depends on the underlying cause of the exanthem.
