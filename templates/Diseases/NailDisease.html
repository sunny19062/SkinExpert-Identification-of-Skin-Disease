Nail Disease

Overview

Nail diseases are diseases or disorders that affect the nails on the fingers and/or toes. Nail disease symptoms can include discoloration, thickening, ridges, and nail separation from the nail bed. Nail diseases can be caused by a variety of factors, including fungal infections, bacterial infections, and underlying medical conditions.

Symptoms

  • Discoloration
  • Thickening
  • Ridges
  • Nail separation from the nail bed

Treatment

The treatment of nail diseases depends on the underlying cause. Treatments can include topical or oral medications, surgery, and other interventions, depending on the specific condition.
