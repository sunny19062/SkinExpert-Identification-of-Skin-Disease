Cellulitis

Overview

Cellulitis is a bacterial skin infection that commonly affects the legs and feet, but can occur anywhere on the body. It appears as a swollen, red area of skin that feels hot and tender to the touch. Left untreated, the infection can spread rapidly to the lymph nodes and bloodstream, causing severe illness.

Symptoms

  • Swelling
  • Redness
  • Warmth
  • Tenderness
  • Pain or soreness
  • Fever and chills (if the infection spreads)

Treatment

Treatment for cellulitis usually involves a course of antibiotics to eliminate the infection. Pain relievers and anti-inflammatory medications may also be prescribed to manage pain and reduce inflammation.
