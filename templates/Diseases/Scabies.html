Scabies

Overview

Scabies is a highly contagious skin infestation caused by a mite called Sarcoptes scabiei. The mite burrows into the skin, causing an intense itching sensation and a pimple-like rash. Scabies is most commonly spread through close physical contact with an infected person. Treatment typically involves topical creams or oral medications.

Symptoms

  • Intense itching, especially at night
  • Rash, consisting of small red bumps and blisters
  • Thin, irregular, raised tracks made up of tiny blisters or bumps on the skin
  • Sores caused by scratching

Treatment

Treatment typically involves topical creams or oral medications.
