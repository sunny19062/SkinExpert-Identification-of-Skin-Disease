Lupus Chronic

Overview

Lupus is a chronic autoimmune disease that can damage any part of the body, including the skin, joints, and organs. The most common symptoms of lupus are extreme fatigue, skin rashes, joint pain, and unexplained fever. There is no cure for lupus, but medications and lifestyle changes can help control the symptoms.

Symptoms

  • Extreme fatigue
  • Skin rashes
  • Joint pain
  • Unexplained fever

Treatment

There is no cure for lupus, but medications and lifestyle changes can help control the symptoms. Medications used to treat lupus include:

  • Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs)
  • Corticosteroids
  • Immunosuppressants
  • Antimalarials
  • Biologics
