Lichen Planus

Overview

Lichen planus is a skin condition that causes itchy, flat-topped bumps and rough patches on the skin or mucous membranes. The cause is unknown, but it is thought to be related to an overactive immune system. It is not contagious. Lichen planus usually goes away on its own, but if symptoms are severe, treatment may include topical or oral medications.

Symptoms

  • Itching
  • Purplish, flat-topped bumps
  • Rough, scaly patches
  • White, lacy patches on mucous membranes

Treatment

If symptoms are severe, treatment may include topical or oral medications such as corticosteroids, antihistamines, or retinoids.
