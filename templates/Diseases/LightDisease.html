Light Disease

Overview

Light disease is a medical condition in which a person is sensitive to light. This sensitivity can cause a range of symptoms, such as headaches, nausea, dizziness, and skin irritation. Some people may experience these symptoms even when exposed to normal levels of light, while others may only experience symptoms when exposed to bright or flickering light.

Symptoms

  • Headaches
  • Nausea
  • Dizziness
  • Skin irritation

Treatment

There is no cure for light disease, but symptoms can be managed with the following treatments:

  • Avoiding bright or flickering light
  • Wearing protective eyewear
  • Taking over-the-counter pain relievers
  • Using anti-nausea medication
  • Applying soothing creams or lotions to irritated skin
