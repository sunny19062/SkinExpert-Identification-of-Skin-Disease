Erythema Multiforme

Overview

Erythema multiforme is a skin condition characterized by the development of red, raised, and sometimes blistered patches on the skin. It can be triggered by various factors, such as infections, medications, and autoimmune disorders. Symptoms range from mild to severe and can include fever, itching, and joint pain. Treatments include topical and oral medications, as well as management of underlying triggers.

Symptoms

  • red, raised, and sometimes blistered patches on the skin
  • fever
  • itching
  • joint pain

Treatment

Treatments include topical and oral medications, as well as management of underlying triggers.
