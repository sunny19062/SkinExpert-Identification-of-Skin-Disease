Bullous Disease

Overview

Bullous disease is a group of rare skin disorders characterized by the presence of fluid-filled blisters on the skin. There are several types of bullous disease, including pemphigus, bullous pemphigoid, and dermatitis herpetiformis. Symptoms may include intense itching, burning, or stinging, and blisters that may break open and form painful sores. Treatments include corticosteroids and other immunosuppressive drugs.

Symptoms

  • Fluid-filled blisters on the skin
  • Intense itching, burning, or stinging
  • Blisters that may break open and form painful sores

Treatment

Treatments for bullous disease include corticosteroids and other immunosuppressive drugs.
